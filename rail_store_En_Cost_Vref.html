Model documentation

This guide explains every parameter, its physical meaning, how to fill it in, and the underlying calculation formulas. Use alongside the Inputs and Assumptions tabs.

📋 Workflow — How to use the model
  1. Inputs — Project parameters: Fill in gross workshop surface, useful height, and fleet size. These values start the full calculation chain.
  2. Inputs — Storage families: Adjust the shares (%) by family. Use the system profile buttons (Metro, Tram, etc.) to load typical values, then refine. The total must equal 100%.
  3. Assumptions: Review geometry and efficiency parameters. Default values are calibrated for a medium-size Metro depot. Only change what differs from your actual project.
  4. Results: Read recommended installed capacities per family and totals per equipment type (bays, modules, ML, places). These are the installed quantities to specify at pre-design stage.
  5. Layout 2D: Adjust the store building width. Drag and resize zones to test different spatial configurations. Check the footprint / S_net ratio.
🔢 Calculation chain — Formulas
Step 1 — Net storage area (S_net)
▶
Principle

The total workshop surface is reduced in two steps: first we extract the share allocated to the store (k_store), then we remove non-storage areas within that store (k_nostor). The remainder is the net surface available for racks.

Typical values

k_store = 18-22% for a standard railway depot.
k_nostor = 30-40% (receiving 8%, staging 10%, dock 5%, office/HSE 5%, circulation 10-15%).

── Store gross surface ─────────────────────────────── S_gross = S_workshop × k_store = 6 000 m² × 0.18 = 1 080 m² ── Non-storage area (receiving, staging, office…) ──── S_nostor = S_gross × k_nostor = 1 080 × 0.35 = 378 m² ── Net storage area ← BASE OF ALL CALCULATIONS ────── S_net = S_gross − S_nostor = 1 080 − 378 = 702 m² Compact form: S_net = S_workshop × k_store × (1 − k_nostor)
Step 2 — Capacity per storage family
▶
Principle

S_net is split according to each family's share (%). Each raw zone is reduced by a layout efficiency factor (eff) to obtain the actual equipment footprint (aisles deducted). The footprint is then converted to capacity units based on the storage type.

── For each family i ───────────────────────────────── S_raw_i = S_net × share_i ← raw zone allocated to the family = 702 × 0.30 = 210.6 m² (e.g. pallets 30%) S_fp_i = S_raw_i × eff_i ← rack footprint after deducting aisles = 210.6 × 0.45 = 94.8 m² (eff_pallet = 45%) ── Conversion S_fp → capacity (by type) ────────────── Pallet racks : N_pos = S_fp / a_pos × N_lev = 94.8 / 1.1925 × 3 = 238 positions Shelving : ML_sol = S_fp / she_D = 70.2 / 0.50 = 140.4 m linéaires Cantilever : ML = S_fp / (2 × can_a) = 12.3 / 1.60 = 7.7 m linéaires Floor storage : places = S_fp / (flo_W × flo_D) = 45.6 / 4.00 = 11.4 places Dedicated / Repairables: zone = S_fp (m²) ── Applying growth margin ──────────────────────────── Recommended installed = CEILING ( gross_capacity × (1 + k_gr), rounding_step ) e.g. : CEILING ( 238 × 1.10, 1 ) = 262 positions Rounding: 1 for positions and places · 0.5 for ML (rounded to nearest half-metre)
Step 3 — Pallet rack geometry (bay_W, bay_D, a_pos, N_lev)
▶
── Bay dimensions ──────────────────────────────────── bay_W = 2×pal_w + 2×clr_s + clr_b ← total width, 2 pallets side by side = 2×1.20 + 2×0.075 + 0.10 = 2.65 m bay_D = pal_d + clr_d ← rack depth (pallet + tolerance) = 0.80 + 0.10 = 0.90 m a_pos = bay_W × bay_D / pal_bay ← footprint per pallet position (m²/pos) = 2.65 × 0.90 / 2 = 1.1925 m²/pos ── Level count calculation (auto from H_useful) ────── h_lev = pal_h + clr_v ← height per level (load + clearance) = 1.20 + 0.20 = 1.40 m N_lev = INT( (H_useful − clr_bt − clr_tp) / h_lev ) = INT( (6.00 − 0.15 − 0.30) / 1.40 ) = INT( 3.96 ) = 3 levels h_frame = clr_bt + N_lev × h_lev + clr_tp ← check against H_useful = 0.15 + 3×1.40 + 0.30 = 4.65 m (must be ≤ H_useful)
Step 4 — Layout efficiency factor (eff)
▶
Definition

The eff factor represents the share of the raw zone (S_raw) that becomes actual rack footprint after deducting main aisles and cross aisles. It is an empirical parameter based on real layout experience.

Example: pallet rack zone, eff_pal = 0.45 S_fp = S_raw × eff_pal = 210.6 × 0.45 = 94.8 m² The raw zone is 210.6 m². Of these 210.6 m²: · 94.8 m² are occupied by racks (actual footprint) · 115.8 m² are handling aisles (= 55% of the zone) Typical values by storage type: eff_pal = 0.45 ← wide forklift aisles (3-3.5m) eff_she = 0.50 ← narrower pedestrian aisles (0.8-1.0m) eff_can = 0.35 ← wide aisles for crane handling and long items eff_flo = 0.65 ← demarcated places, direct circulation

Principle des parts (%)

Each family receives a fraction of S_net. The sum of all 8 shares must equal exactly 100%. These shares reflect the relative volumetric weight of each parts category in the depot's stock policy.

Target occupancy rates (occ)
▶
Role in the calculation

The target occupancy rate defines the planned fill level of the equipment. Practical capacity = gross capacity × occ. It is not an extra sizing factor on installed capacity — it is a planning indicator for the storekeeper.

ParameterDefaultExplanation
occ_pal — Pallet racks80%Never aim for 100%: margin is needed for emergency receipts, pallets being conditioned, and access to picking positions. 80% is the industry standard rule (FEM 9.831).
occ_she — Shelving80%Same logic. Above 85%, stockers can no longer easily find free slots when putting stock away.
occ_can — Cantilever75%A larger margin is needed because long items are harder to reposition and require maneuvering clearance in the aisle.
occ_flo — Floor storage75%Heavy and bulky parts require permanently clear circulation lanes for the forklift and/or overhead crane.
occ_ded — Dedicated area100%The dedicated zone is sized to match its need exactly. It does not serve as a buffer — leave at 100%.
Layout efficiency factors (eff)
▶
ParameterDefaultRangePhysical meaning
eff_pal0.450.35 – 0.55For 100 m² of pallet zone: 45 m² are racks and 55 m² are handling aisles (main aisle ≈ 3.5m for reach truck, cross aisles ≈ 2m). In an optimized logistics warehouse: 0.50-0.55. In a workshop with column constraints: 0.35-0.40.
eff_she0.500.40 – 0.60Narrower pedestrian aisles (80-100 cm) give better efficiency than pallet racks. 0.50 fits a double-face configuration with shelving on both sides of one aisle. Increase it for open shelving with fewer constraints.
eff_can0.350.30 – 0.45Cantilever aisles must remain wide enough for overhead crane or side-loader handling (aisle ≥ 4m), which lowers efficiency. Reduce to 0.30 if one crane handles most movements.
eff_flo0.650.55 – 0.70Floor places are marked with painted lines, while circulation happens in the remaining aisles. 0.65 fits a row layout with 2.5-3m lanes. Reduce to 0.55 for crane handling with omnidirectional circulation.

SymbolNameDefaultStandard/ReferenceHow to fill in
pal_wPallet width along beam1.20 mISO 6780Pallet dimension in the direction parallel to the beams. EUR pallet 1200×800: pal_w=1.20 m if the 1200 mm run along the beam. Verify the actual loading orientation on the project.
pal_dPallet depth in rack0.80 mISO 6780Pallet dimension in the rack-depth direction (perpendicular to the aisle). EUR pallet: 0.80 m. Industrial pallet 1200×1200 mm: pal_d = 1.20 m, so a_pos must be reviewed.
pal_hAvg loaded pallet height1.20 mEN 15620Total height including empty pallet (144mm) + load. Average value across the entire stock. If the tallest pallets exceed 1.50 m, use 1.50 m to avoid undersizing levels.
clr_sSide clearance (pallet to upright)75 mmEN 15620 cl.300Minimum gap between pallet edge and upright centreline. 75 mm is the EN 15620 value for class 300 trucks (reach truck). Increase to 100 mm for less precise trucks or narrow aisles.
clr_bClearance between pallets in one bay100 mmEN 15620 cl.300Central gap between two adjacent pallets on the same beam. 100 mm minimum per EN 15620. Do not reduce below 75 mm even in constrained space.
clr_dDepth tolerance100 mm—Margin added to pal_d to account for rear overhang, rack structure, and positioning tolerances. 100 mm is conservative — it can be reduced to 75 mm on new, well-calibrated racks.
clr_vVertical clearance between load and beam200 mmEN 15620Minimum clearance between the top of the highest load and the underside of the next beam. 200 mm for standard fork trucks. Increase to 250 mm for unstable pallets or protruding packaging.
clr_btBottom clearance (first beam)150 mmEN 15512Height of the first beam centre above the finished floor. 150 mm minimum to prevent fork contact with rack feet. In wet areas or high-pressure cleaning zones: 200 mm.
clr_tpTop clearance (below structure)300 mmEN 15620Free space between the top of the last loaded level and the first obstacle (structure, sprinkler, lighting). 300 mm minimum. For ESFR sprinklers: check the specific fire calculation note (often 500 mm or more).
pal_bayPallet positions per bay per level2—Standard = 2 (one bay = two pallets side by side). Use 1 only for special racks such as very heavy-duty, double-depth, or cantilever-like layouts.
